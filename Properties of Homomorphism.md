#principle #math/algebra 
## preparation
Let $\phi$ a [[Homomorphism]] of Group $G$ and $G^{\prime}$
Let [[Identity]] of Group Group $G, G^{\prime}$ to be $1_G, 1_{G^{\prime}}$


## conclusion
1. if $a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n \in G$, then $\phi(a_1a_2\dots a_n) = \phi(a_1) \phi(a_2) \dots \phi(a_n)$ 
2. $\phi(1_G)  = 1_{G^{\prime}}$
3. $\phi(a^{-1}) = \phi(a)^{-1}$

## proof
1. Trivial
2. $\phi(1_G 1_G) = \phi(1_G) \phi(1_G) = \phi(1_G)$ so it is trivial $\phi(1_G) = 1_{G^{\prime}}$
3. $\phi(a a^{-1}) = \phi(a) \phi(a^{-1}) = \phi(1_G) = 1_{G^{\prime}}$ so $\phi(a^{-1}) = \phi(a)^{-1}$
