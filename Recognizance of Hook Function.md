## Thoughts

The term $x + \frac{1}{x}$ has an exponent span of 2 and consists of 2 terms. According to the [[Special Structure of Hook function]], squaring it gives $(x+\frac{1}{x})^2 = x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} + 2$, where the exponent span becomes 4.
Based on the properties of fractional identities, it is easy to observe that $f(x + \frac{1}{x}) = \frac{\phi_1(x+\frac{1}{x})}{\phi_2(x + \frac{1}{x})}$. You can construct a symmetric fractional structure by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by the "middle power."

$f(x + \frac{1}{x}) = \frac{\frac{x+x^3}{x^2}}{\frac{1+x^4}{x^2}} = \frac{x+\frac{1}{x}}{(x + \frac{1}{x})^2 - 2}$
