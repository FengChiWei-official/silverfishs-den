### Case 1
Given $y = f(x), x \ne x_0$, $f$ is continuous on the point $x_0$, what is the value of $f(x_0)$?
> $x \ne x_0$ means the expression can't cover $x_0$, not $x_0$ doesn't exist.

**Solution**: $f(x_0) = lim_{x \to x_0} f(x)$
