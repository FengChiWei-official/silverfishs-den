## Definition

$$
y = \phi(x),  x = \phi(\phi(x))
$$

## Common Instances

$y = -x$ and $y = \frac{1}{x}$ are most common Instances.

## Usage

> [!note]
> You can get abstract function from Identity

$$
\alpha_1 f(t) + \alpha_2 f(\phi(t)) = \alpha_3  
$$ 
Then, we can get
$$
\begin{align}
&&&\alpha_1 f(\phi(t)) + \alpha_2 f(\phi(\phi(t))) = \alpha_3  \\
&\to && \alpha_1 f(\phi(t)) + \alpha_2 f(t) = \alpha_3 
\end{align}
$$

These two Identities forms a system to solve $f(x)$.
