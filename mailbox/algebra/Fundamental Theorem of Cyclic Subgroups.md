## Definition
Let $\mathbf{G}$ be a group, and let $x \in \mathbf{G}$.
1. The [[Cyclic Subgroup]] generated by $x$ is denoted as $<x> = \{x^k \mid k \in \mathbb{Z} \}$.
2. Define the set $\mathbf{S} = \{k \mid x^k = 1_{\mathbf{G}}, k \in \mathbb{Z}\}$, consisting of all integers for which the power of $x$ equals the identity element.

The following properties hold:
1. $\mathbf{S}$ is a subgroup of [[Additive Group of Integers]]
2. $x^r = x^s, \text{ let r > s } \text{ if and only if } (r-s) \in \mathbf{S}$
3. Suppose that $\mathbf{S}$ is not trivial, then let $\mathbf{S} = \mathbb{Z}n$, $1, x, x^2, \dots, x^{n-1}$ are distinct elements of $<x>$.
4. we say n is the [[order of cyclic subgroup| order]] of $<x>$
