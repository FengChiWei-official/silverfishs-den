## Text

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{S} &= \mathbb{Z}a + \mathbb{Z}b, \\
d &= \gcd(a, b), \\
(\mathbf{S}, +) &\cong (\mathbb{Z}d, +).
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## Thoughts
### Basis
[[The Cyclic Decomposition Theorem for Subgroups of the Addition Group of Integers]]: (All Sub of $(Z,+)$ is $(Zd, +)$)
### Roadmap
1. Let $\mathbb{Z}a + \mathbb{Z}b$ is a Subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}^+$ 
	1. $\mathbf{S} = \{0\}$
	2. $S \ne \{0\}$
