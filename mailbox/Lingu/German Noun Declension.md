## Definition

Noun declension in German is the set of morphological changes (endings, articles, and sometimes stem changes) that mark case, number, and sometimes gender for nouns and their modifiers.

## Key Patterns

- Strong vs weak declension patterns for masculine and neuter nouns
- Plural formation: many irregular patterns (e.g., -e, -er, -n/en, umlaut changes)
- Interaction with articles and adjectives: endings depend on the presence/type of article

## Example

- Der Hund (Nom.), den Hund (Acc.), dem Hund (Dat.), des Hundes (Gen.)
