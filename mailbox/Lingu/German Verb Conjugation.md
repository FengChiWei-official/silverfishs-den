## Definition

German verb conjugation covers person and number agreement in finite forms, tense and mood marking, and participle formation (for compound tenses). Verbs are classified as regular (weak), irregular (strong), and mixed.

## Quick Points

- Present tense endings vary by person (`ich -e`, `du -st`, `er/sie/es -t`, `wir -en`, `ihr -t`, `sie/Sie -en`).
- Common tenses: present, simple past (preterite), perfect (present perfect), pluperfect, future.
- Modal verbs and separable-prefix verbs have special behaviors.
