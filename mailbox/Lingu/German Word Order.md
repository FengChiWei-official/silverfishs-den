## Definition

German word order is characterized by the V2 (verb-second) constraint in main clauses and verb-final order in subordinate clauses. Positioning of objects, adverbials, and separable prefixes follows systematic patterns that affect emphasis and meaning.

## Key Points

- Main clauses: finite verb usually in second position (Ich sehe den Hund.)
- Yes/no questions and imperatives place the verb first (Kommst du? Komm!).
- Subordinate clauses introduced by subordinating conjunctions put the verb at the end (weil ich den Hund sehe).
