## Definition

The four grammatical cases in German — Nominative, Accusative, Dative, and Genitive — mark the syntactic and semantic roles of noun phrases (subject, direct object, indirect object, and possessive relationships).

## Quick Overview

- Nominative: subject (Der Hund läuft.)
- Accusative: direct object (Ich sehe den Hund.)
- Dative: indirect object (Ich gebe dem Hund Futter.)
- Genitive: possession/relationship (Das Fell des Hundes.)

## Notes

- Many prepositions govern a specific case; some prepositions can take two cases with different meanings.
- Word order and verb position interact with case marking to determine meaning.
