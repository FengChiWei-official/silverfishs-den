#principle #math/algebra/abstract 
## preparation
let:
- $\mathbf{S} = \{k \mid x^k = 1\}$
- $<x>$ cyclic subgroup
## conclusion
1. $\mathbf{S}$ is a subgroup of [[Addition Group]]
2. $x^r = x^s, \text{ let r > s } \text{ if and only if } (r-s) \in \mathbf{S}$
3. Suppose that $\mathbf{S}$ is not trivial, then let $\mathbf{S} = \mathbf{Z}n$, $1, x, x^2, \dots, x^{n-1}$ are distinct elements of $<x>$.
4. we say n is the [[order of cyclic subgroup| order]] of $<x>$
