## Definition
Its notation is $\kappa$.

And it is defined as the magnitude of the derivative of the [[Unit Tangential Vector]] with respect to arc length. It measures the rate at which the [[Unit Tangential Vector]] rotates as one traveres the curve.

$$
\begin {align}
& \kappa  && = \quad \mid \mid \frac{d \hat T} {ds} \mid \mid \\
&&& =  \quad  \mid \kappa \mid \cdot\mid \mid \mathbf{\hat N} \mid \mid
\end {align}
$$

$$
\begin {align}
& \mathbf{a}  && = \frac{d(v \mathbf{\hat T})}{dt} \\
&&& = a_T \mathbf{\hat T} + v \frac{d \mathbf{\hat T}}{ds} \frac{ds}{dt} \\
&&& = a_T \mathbf{\hat T} + v^2 \kappa \mathbf{ \hat N}

\end {align}
$$
 
$$
\begin {align}
& \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{a} && = v^2 \mathbf{\hat T} \times (a_T \mathbf{\hat T} + v \kappa \mathbf{ \hat N}) \\
&&& = v a_T (\mathbf{\hat T} \times \mathbf{\hat T}) + v^3\kappa (\mathbf{\hat T} \times \mathbf{\hat N})\\
&&& = v a_T \mathbf{0} + v^3 \kappa \mathbf{\hat B}\\


\end {align}
$$
