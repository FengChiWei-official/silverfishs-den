## Basis
$1 - \sin^2 x = \cos^2 x$
$1+\tan^2 x = \sec^2 x$
$\sec^2 x - 1 = \tan^2 x$

**Case 1: Difference of Squares** $\sqrt {a^2 - x^2}$

$a^2 - x^2 \ge 0$, so $x \in [-a, a]$, then
$a^2(1 - \frac{x^2}{a^2})$, so $\frac{x}{a} \in [-1, 1]$.

**Case 2: Sum of Squares** $\sqrt {a^2 + x^2}$

$a^2 + x^2 > 0$, so $x \in (-\infty, \infty)$, then
$a^2 (1 + \frac{x^2}{a^2})$.
   
**Case 3: Variable First** $\sqrt{x^2 - a^2}$
