## Definition

It is the reversed form of [[Product to Sum for Triangle]]
let $\alpha = x + y$, $\beta = x - y$, based on *[[Linear Coordinate Transformation]]*.
> $x = \frac {\alpha + \beta}{2}$ $y = \frac {\alpha - \beta}{2}$

$\cos \alpha + \cos \beta = 2 \cos \frac {\alpha + \beta}{2} \cos \frac {\alpha - \beta}{2}$
$\cos \beta - \cos \alpha = 2 \sin \frac {\alpha + \beta}{2} \sin \frac {\alpha - \beta}{2}$
> $2\cos x \cos y = \cos x+y + \cos x-y$ 
> $2 \sin x \sin y = \cos x-y - \cos x+y$

$2\sin \alpha \cos \beta = \sin \frac {\alpha + \beta}{2} + \sin \frac {\alpha - \beta}{2}$
$2\cos \alpha \sin \beta = \sin \frac {\alpha + \beta}{2} - \sin \frac {\alpha - \beta}{2}$
> $2 \sin x \cos y = \sin x+y + \sin x-y$
> $2 \cos x \sin y = \sin x+y - \sin x-y$
