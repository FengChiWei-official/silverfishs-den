## Text



> n.

1. A project is a task that requires a [lot](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/zh/dictionary/english/lot "lot 的释义") of time and effort.
2. A project is a detailed study of a subject by a [pupil](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/zh/dictionary/english/pupil "pupil 的释义") or student.


> v. 

3. To **plan**, to schedule, or to **expect**.
	1. *This sector **is projected to** double in size over the next 12 months.*
4. To project someone or something in a particular way is to **make people see them in that way.**
	1. Bradley **projects** a natural **warmth** and sincerity.
	2. His first job will be **to project** Glasgow **as** a friendly city.
5. You **imagine** that someone has **the same idea or feeling **as you.
	1. you **project** your idea **onto** me!
6. **Someone** projects a film onto screen.
7. To extend, shoot, or thrust outward, forward, or upward beyond a baseline, surface, or main structure .

> [!note] 
> **Image:**
> The brick was deliberately designed by the architect to stretch out 5 inches past > the wall to hold up a beautiful decorative flower pot.
