## Text

### Meaning

*   **Importance or status:** Particularly noticeable, widely known, important, or famous (e.g., *“a prominent politician”* or *“a prominent feature of the landscape”*).
> **adj.**

**standing out**.
1. **physically standing out**: 
	1. projecting, protruding or standing out from its surroundings. --- [[Project v.s. Protrude v.s. Stand out|Ref]]
2. - **Importance or status:** Particularly noticeable, widely known, important, or famous (e.g., “a prominent politician” or “a prominent feature of the landscape”).
#### 2. Synonyms
*   **For physical prominence:** Conspicuous, noticeable, salient, protruding, obtrusive, striking.
*   **For status or importance:** Eminent, distinguished, famous, renowned, celebrated, influential.

#### 3. Etymological Construction (Prefix and Root)
The word comes from the Latin verb *prominere*, meaning "to jut out" or "to project." It is constructed from two main parts:

*   **Prefix: *pro-*** 
    *   **Meaning:** "Forward," "forth," or "out."
*   **Root: *-minere*** 
    *   **Meaning:** "To jut," "to project," or "to overhang." (This root is also found in related words like *eminent* and *imminent*).

**Literal historical meaning:** "Jutting forward" or "standing out." Over time, the physical meaning of "standing out from a surface" expanded metaphorically to include people or ideas that "stand out" in society or importance.
