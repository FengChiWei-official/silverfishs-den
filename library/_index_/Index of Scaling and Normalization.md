## Definition
Scaling and Normalization organizes expressions by dominant factor, denominator form, and dimensionless ratio.

## When to Use

- Numerator and denominator have mixed magnitudes, making dominant-term comparison hard.
- Denominator form blocks cancellation and needs normalization first.
- Power-exponential compositions make order comparison inconvenient.

## Typical Order

1. Extract dominant factors first to unify scale.
2. Normalize the denominator next to open cancellation paths.
3. When needed, move power-exponential forms into the exponential-log framework.
