## Text
### Let
Let x be a element of **finite order** $n$, *which means $<x>$ has n distinct elements*.
Let $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ and be written as $k = q\times n + r, \quad q,r \in \mathbb{Z}, \text{ where } r \in [0, n)$

### Conclusion1
$$x^k = x^r$$
