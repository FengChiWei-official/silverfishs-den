## conclusion
subgroup of $(z,+) \quad \mathbf{Z}$ is always in shape of $\mathbf{Z} a$   , if the subgroup is not the trivial subset $\{0\}$ .
## general strategy
let $S$ be a subgroup of $\mathbf{Z}$ .
let $\mathbf{Z}a = \{n \in Z \mid n = ka \quad \text{for some k in }\mathbf{Z}\}$.

1. if $S$ is not **trivial**, then at least a positive integer must in $S$.
2. shows $\mathbf{Z}a \in S$
3. shows $S \in \mathbf{Z}a$
4. then we know a not trivial $S$ always equals to a $\mathbf{Z}a$
