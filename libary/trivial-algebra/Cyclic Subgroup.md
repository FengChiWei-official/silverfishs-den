## background
for **any** *(no)* binary operator $op$, we can define **power**:
$$
op^x(k)
$$
## Definition
for a [[Group]], $(\mathbf{A}, op)$, we know
$$
A = \{a \mid a = op(x,y),x,y\in\mathbf{A} \text{ and } a \in A \}
$$
if we find a subgroup with **a** integer $k$ that:
$$
<k> = \mathbf{A}^{\prime} = \{a \mid a = \text{op}^x(k), x \in\mathbb{Z}\}
$$
> how to promise $op^0$ is [[Identity]]

$\mathbf{A^{\prime}}$ is a [[Cyclic Subgroup]],
